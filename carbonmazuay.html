About The Project

The Stock Carbon Mazuay REDD+ Project seeks to reduce carbon emissions from deforestation and forest degradation, promoting the conservation and sustainable management of forests in critical preservation areas.

The initiative seeks to contribute to meeting global greenhouse gas (GHG) reduction targets, generate environmental and social benefits for local communities, and preserve the biodiversity of threatened ecosystems.

DESCRIPTION

+
-
The Stock Carbon Mazuay REDD+ Project is an environmental and forest preservation initiative implemented in the Gleba Mazuay, a rural property located in the municipality of Silves, state of Amazonas (AM).
The total area of the property is 989.3423 hectares, of which 966.4202 hectares correspond to Remnant Native Vegetation, representing over 97% of the property’s total area.
The property stands out for its ecologically significant features, particularly its vast native vegetation cover, which contributes to the maintenance of biodiversity and ecosystem services.
As detailed below:
• Permanent Preservation Area (APP): 134.6151 hectares
• Legal Reserve Area (ARL): 854.2886 hectares
The forest inventory conducted in the area identified 18,697 trees, distributed across 76 distinct species, demonstrating the high biodiversity and richness of the Amazon ecosystem present in Gleba Mazuay.
The Stock Carbon Mazuay REDD+ Project aims to preserve and sustainably manage the Gleba Mazuay, aligning efforts to mitigate climate change through the conservation of tropical forests. Over a period of 20 years, the project aims to generate 150,000 carbon credits, significantly contributing to the capture and storage of greenhouse gases, while reinforcing the role of the Amazon as one of the planet's largest climate regulators.
This initiative is an example of how environmental conservation can be integrated with the economic valuation of rural properties, generating ecological, social, and economic benefits, while emphasizing the importance of preserving the Amazon forests for global sustainability.

MONITORING REPORT

+
-
Stock Capital will present a detailed monitoring report aimed at highlighting the climate, social, and environmental impacts of its projects certified by the VCS REDD+ and CCB Verra standards.
The report will include the following points:
• Climate Results: Avoided or removed emissions, quantitative data on carbon capture and storage, and the methodologies used for calculation.

• Environmental Impacts: Information on biodiversity conservation, the recovery of degraded ecosystems, and the assessment of environmental quality

• Social Benefits: Indicators on income generation, capacity building for local communities, and improvements in living conditions.

• Compliance: Results from external audits, certifications, and validation by independent parties, ensuring transparency, the integrity of results, and adherence to established standards.

BLOCKCHAIN GOVERNANCE

+
-
We have developed a Transparency Portal based on Blockchain to ensure maximum trust and security for our stakeholders in greenhouse gas (GHG) offset initiatives and carbon credit acquisition.
The platform offers the following features:
• Immutable tracking: Each carbon credit can be tracked from its origin to final compensation.

• Prevention of duplication: A cryptographic system that prevents the repeated use of carbon credits.

• Guaranteed authenticity: Monitoring data, audits, and validations are recorded transparently and can be verified.

• Advanced governance: We provide auditable reports aligned with best ESG practices, strengthening the trust of investors and regulators.

• Transparent engagement: The data available on the blockchain allows investors, consumers, and other stakeholders to monitor, in real time, the GHG offset actions and climate initiatives, promoting greater transparency, accountability, and a closer relationship with stakeholders.

Real Time Monitoring

Stock Capital recognizes the importance of real-time monitoring to ensure the effectiveness of environmental and forest preservation actions. Using the most advanced technologies, we conduct precise analyses that allow us to detect and prevent illegal deforestation, control unauthorized forest management, identify fire hotspots early, and monitor other critical factors. We use real-time satellite images as well as images from specific time periods, enabling the tracking of land surface changes over time.

The information is sourced from satellites such as Sentinel-1 (synthetic aperture radar) and Sentinel-2 (multispectral optical cameras), which provide information on various aspects such as land use, vegetation, water quality, among others. This continuous monitoring capability enhances the transparency of preservation actions, providing greater agility in decision-making, allowing for quick and effective adjustments in conservation strategies.
